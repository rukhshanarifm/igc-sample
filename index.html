Benefit: Encourages battery storage adoption, reduces evening peak load stress, and provides fair compensation aligned with grid needs.

Expected Outcome: Better grid stability, 20-25% reduction in peak load stress, and more sustainable renewable integration.

Deep Dive: FBR Risk Management System Evaluation

üö¢ IGC Intervention #3: Customs Risk Management Optimization

Status: Actively Being Worked On

Out of 8 planned interventions at Pakistan's customs ports, IGC is actively working on improving FBR's automated risk management system. The system was found to have a critically high false positive rate, incorrectly flagging low-risk shipments as high-risk.

üîç How the System Works

üìã Step 1: Goods Declaration (GD)

When an importer brings goods into Pakistan, they file a Goods Declaration (GD) - a formal document declaring:

  • What goods are being imported
  • Quantity and value
  • Country of origin
  • HS Code (product classification)
  • Applicable duties and taxes

‚ö†Ô∏è The Problem: Some traders deliberately misdeclare goods to avoid high tariffs. For example: declaring "electric fans" (10% duty) instead of "soap" (25% duty) to save on taxes. Or undervaluing imported electronics to reduce customs payments.

ü§ñ Step 2: Risk Management System (Backend)

FBR's automated system analyzes every GD in real-time using:

  • Historical Data: Past declarations from this trader
  • Price Benchmarks: Expected prices for declared goods
  • Pattern Matching: Anomalies in quantity, origin, or HS code
  • Risk Scoring: ML algorithm assigns risk score (0-100)

Classification:

  • Low Risk (Green Channel): Cleared automatically within minutes
  • Medium Risk (Yellow Channel): Document verification required (1-2 hours)
  • High Risk (Red Channel): Physical inspection required (1-3 days delay)

‚ùå The False Positive Problem

IGC's Key Finding: FBR's system has an extremely high false positive rate - meaning many shipments flagged as "high risk" are actually legitimate low-risk imports.

Impact:

  • üïê Trader Time Wasted: Honest businesses face 1-3 day delays for unnecessary physical inspections
  • üí∞ Economic Costs: Storage fees, demurrage charges, and lost business opportunities
  • üëÆ FBR Resources Wasted: Customs officers spend time inspecting low-risk shipments instead of focusing on actual fraud
  • üìâ Efficiency Loss: Only 15-20% of flagged "high-risk" shipments actually contain misdeclarations
  • üò§ Trust Erosion: Businesses lose confidence in the system, some resort to informal channels

Expected Impact: Reduce average clearance time from 2.5 days to 4 hours for compliant traders. Save ‚Ç®8-10 billion annually in economic costs.

Expected Impact: Reduce unnecessary inspections by 25-30%, improve officer efficiency and morale.
